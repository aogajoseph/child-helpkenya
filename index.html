About Us

Who We Are

Child-Help Kenya is a Nonprofit organization that promotes a fair start for children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya through access to healthcare thereby improving their quality of life.

Mission

To empower a generation where children with Spina Bifida and Hydrocephalus become thriving adults in an inclusive society.

Vision

To ensure that all children with Spina Bifida and Hydrocephalus get a fair start by 2050.

Research Objective

  • Developing a Research and Development (R&D) institutional capacity allowing the public to better understand Child-Help Kenya's role and enhance a knowledge-based and scientific approach to Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya.

      • Fundraising Objective

      • Developing co-funding mechanisms to help local partners achieve sustainability by 2030.

          • Partnership Objective

          • Working with stakeholders to form sustainable partnerships.

Service Objectives

  • Working with partners in training neurosurgeons on Endoscopic Third Ventriculostomy (ETV) locally.
  • Providing medical materials e.g., shunts, intermittent catheters, bowel wash-out kits, oxybutinin, cones and flexible scopes for treatment and lifelong care.
  • Ensuring families from far-off areas have access to treatment through the Houses Of Hope project.
  • Preventing pressure sores and kidney damage in children with Spina bifida and promoting inclusion in the society.
  • Working with partners to provide surgeries for children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus.
  • Conducting medical follow-ups through our mobile clinics and Community Based Rehabilitation exercises
  • Capacity building for parents and youth in support groups for mandate execution.
  • Working with partners to prevent future occurences of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus through immunization and food fortification.
  • Public sensitization on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus through publicity, policy advocacy and stakeholder engagement.

Our Causes

Projects

Houses Of Hope

We ensure that families of children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus travelling from far-off areas seeking treatment are accommodated, fed and trained free of charge.

Mobile Clinics

We examine health conditions of children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in our widespread mobile outreach sites located in more than 40 counties in Kenya.

Support Groups

We coordinate trainings, medical interventions and lifelong care practices for children with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus through disability, advocacy and lobby groups in Kenya.

What is Spina bifida?

Spina bifida is a neural tube defect that occurs in the early stages of pregnancy where the neural tube (the structure that develops to form the brain and spine) in an embryo fails to form or close properly.


What is Hydrocephalus?

Hydrocephalus is a condition where an abnormal build-up of Cerebrospinal (CSF) fluid is trapped in the brain. Naturally, CSF flows through the ventricles (a network of cavities within the brain) as a nutrient delivery and waste removal system for the brain. It bathes the brain and spinal cord, protecting and cushioning them from injury, then reabsorbed into the bloodstream.
